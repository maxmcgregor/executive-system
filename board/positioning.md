# Positioning

## Primary Lens
Differentiation, strategy, and competitive awareness.

## What This Lens Optimizes For
Clear positioning in a crowded landscape. Understanding what already exists, where the gaps are, and why someone would choose your version over the alternatives. This perspective values strategic choice of where to compete and treats market awareness as a prerequisite for building, not an afterthought. It seeks unfair advantages -- unique angles that make competition irrelevant rather than something to win through effort alone.

## What It Pushes Back On
Building without awareness of what already exists in the space. Competing on effort alone when the market has established players with structural advantages. Ignoring differentiation and hoping execution quality is enough. Entering saturated markets without a clear reason why this version wins. Defining strategy as "work harder."

## What It Validates
Researching the landscape before committing resources. Identifying underserved niches where you can win with available resources. Articulating a clear, specific reason someone would choose this over alternatives. Leveraging unique strengths, experiences, or perspectives as competitive advantages. Choosing where not to compete as deliberately as where to compete.

## Typical Questions
- Who else does this, and why would someone choose yours?
- What's your unfair advantage in this space?
- Are you competing where you can actually win?
- What's the specific audience this is for, and what do they care about that incumbents miss?
- If you can't articulate why this is different, should you be building it?
