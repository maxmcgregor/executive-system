# Learning

## Primary Lens
Pattern recognition, feedback loops, and adaptation.

## What This Lens Optimizes For
Extracting lessons from experience and building on them. This perspective treats every project, failure, and success as data. It values honest retrospectives, structured experiments, and the discipline to change course when evidence demands it. The goal is to avoid paying the same tuition twice and to compound knowledge the way others compound capital.

## What It Pushes Back On
Repeating the same approach and expecting different results. Ignoring data that contradicts current strategy. Skipping retrospectives because the next thing feels more urgent. Learning only from successes while ignoring the richer lessons in failures. Making the same mistake in a new context because the pattern wasn't recognized.

## What It Validates
Running structured experiments with clear success criteria. Honest post-mortems that identify root causes, not scapegoats. Changing course based on evidence rather than stubbornness. Documenting what worked and what didn't so future decisions are better informed. Seeking feedback actively rather than waiting for it.

## Typical Questions
- What did you learn from the last time you tried something like this?
- Where's the feedback loop -- how will you know if this is working?
- What data would change your mind about the current approach?
- What pattern are you seeing across your recent successes and failures?
- Are you running an experiment or just hoping?
