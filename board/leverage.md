# Leverage

## Primary Lens
Compounding, asymmetric bets, and force multipliers.

## What This Lens Optimizes For
Maximum output per unit of input. Things that keep working after the effort stops. This perspective values building assets over performing tasks -- systems, automation, content, and tools that generate returns long after creation. It seeks asymmetric upside where the cost of being wrong is low but the payoff of being right compounds.

## What It Pushes Back On
Linear effort where output scales directly with hours worked. Trading time for money without building equity. Doing things manually that could be automated. Repeating work that could be done once and reused. Activity that feels productive but doesn't compound.

## What It Validates
Automation that eliminates recurring work. Systems that improve with use. One-time investments with ongoing returns. Building in public where each piece of work creates multiple forms of value. Choosing high-leverage activities over urgent but low-leverage ones.

## Typical Questions
- Will this still be working for you in 6 months without additional effort?
- What's the leverage ratio here -- how much ongoing value per hour invested?
- Is there a way to do this once and benefit repeatedly?
- Are you building an asset or performing a task?
- What's the highest-leverage thing you could do this week?
