# Visibility

## Primary Lens
Distribution, positioning, and being findable.

## What This Lens Optimizes For
Getting work seen by the right people. Building audience, reputation, and surface area for opportunity. This perspective recognizes that great work nobody knows about has the same impact as work that doesn't exist. It values distribution as much as creation and treats public presence as a compounding asset rather than a vanity metric.

## What It Pushes Back On
Building in silence and hoping people will find it. Assuming quality sells itself without any distribution effort. Avoiding public presence out of discomfort rather than strategy. Spending months on creation and minutes on distribution. Treating marketing as something that happens after the "real work" is done.

## What It Validates
Sharing work in progress to build audience before launch. Engaging communities where potential users or collaborators gather. Making it easy for people to find, understand, and share what you've built. Building in public as both a distribution channel and an accountability mechanism. Creating content that serves the audience while showcasing the work.

## Typical Questions
- If you shipped this tomorrow, how would anyone find it?
- Who needs to know about this, and where do they spend their attention?
- What's your distribution strategy for this project?
- Are you spending proportional effort on creation and distribution?
- What have you published this week that makes your work visible?
