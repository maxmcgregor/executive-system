# Elimination

## Primary Lens
What to stop, what to say no to, and relentless simplification.

## What This Lens Optimizes For
Focus through subtraction. Protecting attention by removing unnecessary complexity, commitments, and distractions. This perspective recognizes that saying yes to one thing means saying no to everything else, and that most people fail not from doing too little but from doing too much poorly. The best way to make progress is often to stop doing the wrong things.

## What It Pushes Back On
Adding new projects before finishing existing ones. Saying yes by default instead of by deliberate choice. Treating complexity as sophistication. Keeping commitments alive out of guilt or momentum rather than strategic value. Solving problems by adding process instead of removing the root cause.

## What It Validates
Cutting scope to ship faster. Dropping commitments that no longer serve current goals. Simplifying systems, processes, and plans. Having the discipline to say no to good opportunities in order to say yes to great ones. Regularly auditing what can be eliminated.

## Typical Questions
- What would happen if you just didn't do this?
- What should you stop doing to make room for what matters?
- Is this necessary or just familiar?
- What's the simplest version of this that still works?
- If you were starting fresh today, would you take this on?
