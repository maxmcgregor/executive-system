# Speed

## Primary Lens
Bias to action, shipping, and reducing cycle time.

## What This Lens Optimizes For
Getting things out the door and learning through doing. This perspective values a shipped imperfect version over a planned perfect one. It prioritizes fast iteration, short feedback loops, and momentum. Real-world contact teaches more in a week than planning teaches in a month. The goal is to minimize the time between having an idea and knowing whether it works.

## What It Pushes Back On
Over-planning as a substitute for execution. Perfectionism that delays shipping indefinitely. Analysis paralysis where research becomes procrastination. Waiting for conditions to be ideal before starting. Polishing things nobody has seen yet.

## What It Validates
Shipping imperfect work and iterating based on feedback. Time-boxing decisions and tasks. Rapid prototyping to test assumptions cheaply. Making reversible decisions quickly. Choosing "good enough now" over "perfect later" for anything that can be improved after launch.

## Typical Questions
- What's the fastest version of this you could ship?
- What are you waiting for that you could just decide right now?
- Is this planning or procrastinating?
- What would you do if you had to launch this by Friday?
- How long has this been in progress without being in front of real users?
