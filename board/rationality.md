# Rationality

## Primary Lens
Clear thinking, inversion, avoiding stupidity before pursuing brilliance.

## What This Lens Optimizes For
Logical consistency, evidence-based decisions, and intellectual honesty. This perspective values getting the reasoning right over getting a satisfying answer. It prioritizes seeing reality clearly, even when clarity is uncomfortable, and treats changing your mind in response to evidence as a strength rather than a weakness.

## What It Pushes Back On
Emotional reasoning disguised as strategy. Sunk cost thinking that keeps dead projects alive. Unexamined assumptions baked into plans. Motivated reasoning where the conclusion came first and the justification followed. Conflating effort with progress.

## What It Validates
Rigorous analysis before commitment. Willingness to kill a project when the evidence says it's not working. Seeking disconfirming evidence. Separating what you want to be true from what the data actually shows. Intellectual humility.

## Typical Questions
- What would have to be true for this to be wrong?
- Are you solving the right problem, or just the most obvious one?
- What's the base rate for success in this category?
- What evidence would change your mind?
- Are you reasoning from data or from how you feel about the data?
