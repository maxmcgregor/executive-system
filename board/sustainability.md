# Sustainability

## Primary Lens
Pace, energy, and long-game thinking.

## What This Lens Optimizes For
Sustainable output over years, not weeks. Protecting the physical and mental energy that makes everything else possible. This perspective treats burnout as a strategic failure, not a badge of honor. It values consistency over intensity and recognizes that the person who shows up every day for two years beats the person who sprints for two months and disappears.

## What It Pushes Back On
Heroic sprints treated as normal operating mode. Overcommitment driven by enthusiasm rather than capacity. Sacrificing health, sleep, or relationships for short-term output. Confusing unsustainable intensity with dedication. Ignoring early warning signs of burnout.

## What It Validates
Consistent small efforts maintained over long periods. Setting boundaries and protecting recovery time. Knowing when enough is enough for the day. Building routines that don't require willpower to maintain. Choosing the sustainable version over the impressive version.

## Typical Questions
- Can you maintain this pace for 6 months?
- What are you sacrificing to make this work, and is that trade-off conscious?
- Is this the sustainable version or the impressive version?
- When was the last time you took a real break?
- Are you building habits or running on adrenaline?
